SECTION 1: VESTAS V150-4.5 MW — OPERATIONS & FAULT REFERENCE
Manufacturer: Vestas Wind Systems A/S
Model: V150-4.5 (4,500 kW rated output)
Rotor Diameter: 150 m  |  Hub Height: 105–166 m  |  Cut-in: 3 m/s  |  Cut-out: 25 m/s
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  PERFORMANCE OVERVIEW

The V150-4.5 is designed for low-to-medium wind speed sites (IEC Wind Class IIA–IIIA).
At a reference wind speed of 8.5 m/s, rated output is typically achieved. The power
curve achieves 50% rated output at approximately 6.8 m/s hub-height wind speed. Annual
availability targets are 97% for turbines within the first 10 years of operation.

Any sustained deviation of more than 15% below the expected power curve at a given
wind speed — sustained over a rolling 7-day window — should trigger a performance
investigation. Acceptable causes include icing, planned curtailment, or grid limitation.
Unexplained deviations must be escalated to the operations centre.

1.2  FAULT CODES — DRIVETRAIN SYSTEM

FAULT CODE: E-1001
Name: Gearbox Overheating
Trigger: Gearbox oil temperature sensor reading exceeds 80°C, or coolant flow rate
         drops below 4.2 L/min.
Severity: HIGH — turbine will perform automatic shutdown if temperature reaches 90°C.
Symptoms: Increased noise from nacelle, oil temperature trending upward over 2–4 hours,
          possible reduction in output power as protection ramp-down activates.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Perform immediate remote inspection via SCADA. Check oil temperature trend.
  2. If temperature below 85°C and stable: schedule on-site inspection within 48 hours.
  3. If temperature above 85°C or rising: initiate controlled shutdown. Do not restart
     until coolant system is inspected by a certified technician.
  4. On-site: inspect coolant reservoir level, pump operation, and heat exchanger for
     blockage. Replace gearbox oil filter (Part No. VW-OIL-F42) if last replacement
     exceeds 4,000 operational hours.
  5. After repair: perform 30-minute warm-up run at reduced load before returning to
     full production.
Estimated Repair Time: 4–8 hours (filter replacement) / 2–5 days (pump replacement).

FAULT CODE: E-1002
Name: Rotor Imbalance / Blade Pitch Deviation
Trigger: Pitch angle deviation between any two blades exceeds 2.0°, measured over a
         sustained 60-second window at wind speeds above 6 m/s.
Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH — continued operation risks accelerated bearing wear and fatigue
          loading on the main shaft.
Symptoms: Increased tower shadow effect, abnormal vibration at 1P frequency,
          asymmetric power output visible in SCADA 10-minute data.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Check pitch controller log for recent calibration events or actuator errors.
  2. Verify pitch encoder readings against physical blade position (requires on-site visit).
  3. Recalibrate pitch actuators using the Vestas Pitch Calibration Tool (PCT v3.1).
  4. If blade damage is suspected (lightning strike, erosion), perform visual inspection.
     Use drone inspection if hub height exceeds 100 m.
  5. If deviation persists after calibration: replace pitch actuator (Part No. VW-ACT-P15).
Estimated Repair Time: 6–10 hours (calibration) / 1–2 days (actuator replacement).

FAULT CODE: E-4001
Name: Yaw System Fault — Heading Error
Trigger: Nacelle heading deviates from optimal wind direction by more than 10° sustained
         over 5 minutes, with wind speeds above 5 m/s.
Severity: MEDIUM — causes AEP loss typically between 2–8% depending on sustained misalignment.
Symptoms: SCADA yaw error alarm, visible nacelle misalignment on webcam, power output
          10–20% below expected at current wind speed.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Check yaw drive motor current; compare against baseline (nominal: 12–14 A at rated speed).
  2. Inspect yaw gear teeth for wear or foreign objects.
  3. Verify wind vane calibration — replace if last calibration >12 months.
  4. If yaw motor fault code present: replace yaw motor (Part No. VW-YAW-M08).
  5. Perform yaw calibration procedure after any hardware replacement.
Estimated Repair Time: 3–6 hours (calibration) / 1 day (motor replacement).

FAULT CODE: E-4002
Name: Blade Pitch Actuator Slow Response — Emergency Feather
Trigger: Pitch actuator response time exceeds 8 seconds for a commanded 5° pitch change
         during a wind speed ramp event above 12 m/s.
Severity: HIGH — automatic emergency feather (blades to 90°) is engaged. Turbine stops.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Do not attempt remote restart. On-site inspection required.
  2. Check hydraulic pressure in pitch actuator circuit (nominal: 180–200 bar).
  3. Inspect hydraulic hose connections and accumulator pre-charge pressure.
  4. Replace hydraulic pump seal kit if internal leakage suspected (Part No. VW-HYD-S22).
  5. After repair, perform pitch response test per procedure in Section 7.4 of the full manual.
Estimated Repair Time: 8–16 hours.

SECTION 2: SIEMENS GAMESA SG-6.6-170 — OPERATIONS & FAULT REFERENCE
Manufacturer: Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy
Model: SG-6.6-170 (6,600 kW rated output)
Rotor Diameter: 170 m  |  Hub Height: 115–165 m  |  Cut-in: 3 m/s  |  Cut-out: 25 m/s
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  PERFORMANCE OVERVIEW

The SG-6.6-170 is Siemens Gamesa's flagship onshore turbine optimised for medium-to-high
wind speed sites (IEC Class IA–IIA). The integrated SG PowerMode system provides adaptive
output control, allowing temporary overproduction up to 7.2 MW under favourable conditions.

Expected capacity factor on a Class II site: 38–45%. Rolling 30-day availability below
95% should trigger a technical review. Performance deviations exceeding 12% from the
reference power curve at wind speeds between 8–14 m/s are classified as critical anomalies
requiring investigation within 72 hours.

2.2  FAULT CODES — ELECTRICAL AND CONTROL SYSTEM

FAULT CODE: E-1003
Name: Generator Bearing Vibration Elevated
Trigger: Vibration sensor at generator drive-end bearing exceeds 8.0 mm/s RMS (velocity),
         or 0.5 g peak acceleration for more than 60 continuous seconds.
Severity: HIGH — prolonged operation risks bearing failure and secondary damage to generator windings.
Symptoms: Audible noise increase in nacelle (high-pitched whine), SCADA vibration alarm,
          possible slight increase in generator temperature.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Reduce turbine load to 60% rated output via SCADA curtailment to reduce bearing stress.
  2. Monitor vibration trend over 4-hour window at reduced load.
  3. If vibration decreases below 5.0 mm/s: schedule bearing inspection within 7 days.
  4. If vibration remains above 6.0 mm/s at curtailed load: initiate controlled shutdown.
  5. On-site: collect grease sample for laboratory analysis. Replace bearing if wear particles
     detected (Part No. SG-BRG-GDE6). Estimated Repair Time: 1–3 days.

FAULT CODE: E-2002
Name: Power Curve Deviation
Trigger: Turbine output is more than 15% below the reference power curve at the measured
         hub-height wind speed, averaged over a 6-hour window.
Severity: MEDIUM — indicates aerodynamic or mechanical performance degradation.
Possible Root Causes:
  a) Blade contamination (insects, leading edge erosion) — common in coastal locations.
  b) Blade pitch offset — see E-1002.
  c) Air density variation — check if site temperature and pressure have changed significantly.
  d) Partial grid curtailment by TSO — verify in SCADA event log.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Confirm curtailment is not active in SCADA.
  2. Check blade pitch angles at standstill vs commissioned values.
  3. If blade erosion suspected: schedule drone or rope-access inspection.
  4. Leading edge erosion repair typically recovers 3–6% AEP.

SECTION 3: LONGI SOLAR HI-MO 6 — INVERTER & PLANT FAULT REFERENCE
Manufacturer: LONGi Solar / SMA Solar Technology (inverter)
Panel Model: Hi-MO 6 (mono-PERC, 580 Wp per panel)
Inverter: SMA Sunny Central 4200 UP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1  PERFORMANCE OVERVIEW

The Hi-MO 6 panels are rated at STC (1000 W/m², 25°C cell temperature, AM1.5).
In field conditions, actual output depends on irradiance, temperature coefficient
(−0.29%/°C above 25°C), and soiling losses (typically 2–5% in semi-arid Spain).

Performance Ratio (PR) is the primary KPI for solar plants. Expected PR: 79–84%.
A PR below 75% sustained over 7 consecutive days signals a plant-level anomaly.
PR is calculated as: PR = E_actual / (G_poa × P_stc) where G_poa is plane-of-array
irradiance in kWh/m² and P_stc is the installed DC capacity.

3.2  FAULT CODES — INVERTER AND STRING SYSTEM

FAULT CODE: E-3001
Name: Inverter Overtemperature
Trigger: Inverter cabinet internal temperature exceeds the threshold defined as:
         ambient temperature + 45°C delta T, sustained for more than 10 minutes.
Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH — thermal protection will throttle output or trip unit.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Check inverter air filters — clean or replace if blocked (schedule: every 6 months).
  2. Verify cooling fans are operational; replace failed fans within 24 hours.
  3. If ambient temperature is exceptionally high (>40°C), temporary derating is normal.
  4. If fault persists after filter cleaning: check IGBT module thermal interface material
     (Part No. SMA-IGBT-4200). Replace if thermal resistance has increased.
Estimated Repair Time: 2–4 hours (filter/fan) / 1 day (IGBT module).

FAULT CODE: E-3002
Name: String Underperformance
Trigger: DC string current is more than 20% below expected given current irradiance,
         averaged over a 30-minute window with irradiance above 400 W/m².
Severity: MEDIUM — typically indicates partial shading, soiling, or panel/string fault.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Review I-V curve trace for affected string (requires on-site string tester).
  2. Check for physical shading (new obstructions, vegetation growth).
  3. Perform electroluminescence (EL) imaging to detect cracked or delaminated panels.
  4. Clean affected string if soiling is confirmed as root cause.
  5. Replace defective panels if EL imaging identifies cell failure (Part process: OEM RMA).

FAULT CODE: E-3003
Name: Grid Frequency Out of Tolerance — Automatic Disconnect
Trigger: Grid frequency deviates beyond 47.5–51.5 Hz for more than 200 milliseconds.
Severity: HIGH — inverter performs automatic safe disconnection per EN 50549-1.
         This is a protective response, not a hardware failure.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Check national grid operator (REE in Spain) for system-wide frequency events.
  2. If grid event confirmed: await grid normalisation; inverter will reconnect automatically.
  3. If frequency was within tolerance but fault triggered: recalibrate frequency relay
     (calibration procedure in SMA Sunny Central service manual, Section 12).
  4. Log the event in Horizon for regulatory reporting purposes.

FAULT CODE: E-5001
Name: Transformer Oil Temperature Warning
Trigger: MV transformer oil temperature exceeds 85°C (Warning) or 95°C (Alarm/Trip).
Severity: MEDIUM (Warning) / HIGH (Alarm)
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Verify transformer cooling fans are operating at correct speed.
  2. Check transformer oil level via sight glass.
  3. If oil temperature exceeds 90°C: reduce plant output to 70% via SCADA.
  4. If 95°C is reached: the protection relay will trip the transformer. Do not reset
     until temperature drops below 75°C and root cause is identified.
  5. Contact transformer OEM if overheat occurs without clear environmental cause.

FAULT CODE: E-5002
Name: Main Circuit Breaker Trip — Overvoltage Protection
Trigger: MV bus voltage exceeds 115% of nominal (typically >690 V on a 600 V bus)
         for more than 500 milliseconds.
Severity: HIGH — automatic protection trip. Plant is offline until manually reset.
Recommended Corrective Actions:
  1. Do not attempt remote reset until voltage on MV bus is confirmed normal.
  2. Contact grid operator to check for upstream voltage events.
  3. On-site: inspect surge arrestors and check for damage to MV cable insulation.
  4. Reset breaker only with authorised personnel on-site.
  5. Log event and report to grid operator per connection agreement obligations.

SECTION 4: GENERAL OPERATIONS GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  MAINTENANCE SCHEDULING

Scheduled maintenance intervals for wind turbines in the GreenPowerMonitor fleet:
  - Semi-annual service (every 6 months): mandatory for all turbines under warranty.
    Includes: gearbox oil sampling, blade visual inspection, torque check of structural
    bolts, lubrication of yaw and pitch bearings, SCADA sensor calibration.
  - Annual full service: includes all semi-annual tasks plus generator alignment check,
    transformer oil sampling, safety system functional tests, and cable inspection.
  - Post-fault inspection: required within 5 working days of any HIGH severity fault event.

For solar plants:
  - Bi-annual panel cleaning in arid environments (more frequent if dustfall >2 g/m²/month).
  - Annual inverter maintenance: filter replacement, fan inspection, I-V curve sweep.
  - Transformer oil sampling: annually for units above 2 MVA.

4.2  DATA QUALITY AND SCADA GAPS

SCADA communication interruptions (fault E-2001) must be logged. Data gaps exceeding
4 hours should be flagged in reporting and excluded from availability calculations
unless the underlying cause is a planned maintenance activity.

Assets with more than 72 cumulative hours of SCADA data gaps per month are classified
as 'monitored with data quality issues' and must be reviewed by the asset manager.

4.3  ESCALATION MATRIX

  Level 1 (Remote response, <1 hour): All MEDIUM faults. Operations centre handles
          via SCADA or remote reset.
  Level 2 (On-site within 48 hours): HIGH severity faults where the turbine is still
          producing at reduced output, or MEDIUM faults recurring >3 times in 30 days.
  Level 3 (Emergency on-site, <8 hours): HIGH severity faults causing full shutdown,
          any safety system activation, or transformer/grid protection trips.
